SECURITY QUESTIONNAIRE RESPONSE
Submitted by Aperture Cloud Security Team

Q: Is customer data encrypted in transit and at rest?
A: Yes. TLS 1.2 or higher is enforced for data in transit. AES-256 is used for encryption at rest.

Q: What is the breach notification commitment?
A: Aperture Cloud will notify Northstar Bank without undue delay and no later than twenty-four hours after confirmation of a security incident affecting customer data.

Q: Does the service support SSO?
A: Yes. SAML 2.0 is supported for enterprise customers. SCIM user provisioning is also available.

Q: Where will Northstar Bank production data reside?
A: The production environment for Northstar Bank is proposed in the European Union region.

Q: Are subprocessors used?
A: Yes. Aperture Cloud uses subprocessors for infrastructure, email delivery, and monitoring. New subprocessors are announced in advance.

Q: How long are audit logs retained?
A: Standard enterprise audit log retention is thirteen months.

Q: What are the disaster recovery objectives?
A: RTO is four hours and RPO is fifteen minutes.

Q: Is penetration testing performed?
A: Yes. Independent penetration testing is performed annually and material findings are tracked to closure.

Q: Does support personnel access customer data?
A: Access is restricted to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis and is logged. Access from outside the approved support region requires a ticket and manager approval.
